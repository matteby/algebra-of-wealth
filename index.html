Chapter 2: The Silent Thief

Alex is now 30. He has a great career and is saving aggressively. But he meets a financial advisor who says, "Investing is too hard. Let me manage your money. I only charge a small 1.5% fee every year."

It sounds tiny. But fees are a percentage of your total wealth, and they destroy the compound interest that money would have earned.

Chapter 3: The Danger of "Sure Things"

Alex is 45. He's frustrated that his wealth isn't growing fast enough. His neighbor tells him about a revolutionary new company that "can't lose." Historically, maybe it was a company called Enron.

Alex breaks the cardinal rule of Diversification. He takes all his money out of his broad index funds and puts it into this one single stock.

Alex loses 99% of his capital.

Chapter 4: The Math of Loss

Alex is devastated. He asks, "How much do I need this stock to bounce back just so I can break even?"

The math of loss is brutal and asymmetrical. If you lose 50%, you don't need a 50% gain to recover. You need a 100% gain.
